# Monetization Roadmap

The first ship is a free useful tool. No paywall, no email capture, no popups. The path to revenue is paved by traffic, not gates. Below are the realistic options to layer in once there is meaningful daily use, in order of expected effort.

## Path 1: sportsbook affiliate links

Industry rates as of mid 2025 (verify when you sign up):

1. DraftKings affiliate: roughly $250 per first time depositor.
2. FanDuel affiliate: roughly $150 per first time depositor.
3. BetMGM affiliate: roughly $100 to $200 per first time depositor depending on geography.
4. Caesars affiliate: roughly $100 to $150 per first time depositor.

Implementation, when the time comes:

1. Apply for each program. Most require a working site with traffic and content (we have both).
2. Wrap the "Best over price at FanDuel" pill so it links to the prop on FanDuel via the affiliate URL.
3. Track conversions through the affiliate dashboard. Add a one line UTM param so we can see which book gets the most clicks.
4. Expected revenue at scale: 1,000 daily users with a 0.5 percent click to deposit conversion at $150 average bounty equals roughly $750 per day. Real numbers are usually 2 to 5 times lower in practice. Plan conservatively.

The honest pitch: we already show the best price. Linking out is a small UX improvement for users and a revenue lever for us. Required to disclose in the footer.

## Path 2: premium tier

Triggers: at least three months of clean accuracy data, hit rate above 53 percent on flagged leans (the breakeven on standard juice), and a recurring user base.

Pricing idea: $9 per month or $79 per year, no free trial nonsense, cancel anytime.

What to put behind the paywall:

1. Earlier predictions (one hour before tip-off vs the public 30 minute refresh).
2. Less common prop markets (steals, blocks, double doubles, first basket).
3. Player parlay correlation tool (does Maxey OVER and Embiid OVER overlap unhealthily).
4. CSV export for power users.

Critically, leave the core free product unchanged. Premium adds, never subtracts. Free users churn faster than they pay.

## Path 3: free email digest

Triggers: roughly 5,000 unique daily users, a clean unsubscribe flow, a sender domain warmed up.

Format: one email at 3pm ET each playoff day. Subject line is the headline pick of the day. Body is the top five edges of the slate with the projection, the line, and a one sentence reason.

Why this matters: an email list compounds. Even at 2 percent open and 0.5 percent click, a list of 20,000 sends 100 daily clicks straight to whatever we want them looking at next (affiliate links, premium tier, a future product).

## Path 4: paid Discord community

Triggers: validated audience that engages in discussion (look for organic Reddit and Twitter mentions), and a willingness to spend a few hours per day moderating.

Pricing idea: $19 per month, gated channels.

What members get:

1. Access to the model authors during games.
2. A live channel that posts every refresh with the new biggest edges.
3. Member only research notes (e.g. "we adjusted Embiid's CI because of his thigh tendency").

Risk: high effort. Communities die fast when the founder loses interest. Only pursue if it is genuinely fun.

## Things to never do

1. No "guaranteed" or "lock" language anywhere. Ever. It is dishonest and it is the fastest way to lose user trust and run afoul of advertising rules.
2. No reaching out to whales. The whole point of this tool is that the math is the same for everyone.
3. No sending push notifications. Email and a clean web experience are enough.
4. No selling user data. There is no user data to sell because we do not require accounts.

## Sequence

Ship free. Get traffic. Confirm accuracy with public data. Add affiliate links. Add the email digest. Then consider premium. Discord is an option, not a goal.
